Why this route? The full Hwy 1/PCH north is the most spectacular coastal drive on earth — Big Sur alone is worth the trip. North of SF the crowds disappear. By Humboldt you'll feel like you're in a different country. Oregon's coast is dramatically different: wilder, wetter, sea stacks and headlands everywhere. Astoria is the low-key gem at the end.

    Night-by-Night Reservation Strategy
    D1 Big Sur
    Reality: Pfeiffer Big Sur, Kirk Creek, Plaskett Creek, and Limekiln-style sites are probably gone.
    Default to hotel/private lodging. Best backups: Cambria/San Simeon, Big Sur cabins/motels, Carmel/Pacific Grove/Monterey. Keep cancellation alerts running.
    D2 Monterey
    Reality: weekend nights near Big Sur/Carmel are not casual bookings.
    Book a refundable hotel now. Pacific Grove is usually more sane than Cannery Row.
    D3 San Francisco
    Reality: expensive, but easier than coastal camping.
    Book hotel with parking clarity. Mission, Inner Sunset, Hayes Valley, or Richmond/Sunset work better than Union Square for this trip.
    D4 Bodega Bay
    Reality: Sonoma coast camps will still be tight.
    Try Doran Beach, Bodega Dunes, Wright's Beach, then pivot inland to Petaluma/Santa Rosa if needed.
    D5 Fort Bragg
    Reality: hotels become more manageable after the holiday weekend.
    Hotel is the right play here because you want charging and a reset after Highway 1.
    D7 Jedediah Smith
    Reality: still popular, but this is one of the more plausible campground targets.
    Book if open. If not, hold Crescent City hotel and scan Del Norte Coast / Prairie Creek / private RV backups.
    D8 Bandon
    Reality: Bullards Beach is popular; weekday timing helps.
    Try Bullards first, then Cape Blanco, private RV parks, or a Bandon hotel if you want dinner and comfort.
    D9 Newport
    Reality: Friday on the Oregon coast is high-risk.
    Treat Beverly Beach as a cancellation target. Hold Newport/Yachats hotel or South Beach/Beachside/private camp backup.
    D10 Cannon Beach
    Reality: do not assume a campground or cheap hotel.
    Book a refundable room now in Cannon Beach, Manzanita, Seaside, or Astoria. This is a hotel night by design.
    Cancellation scanning cadence: check once in the morning and once in the evening. Increase to 3-5 checks/day in the final 10 days before each stay, when people cancel before penalty windows.
    Reservation rule: every critical night should have a refundable fallback before you chase campsites. Cancel the fallback only after the campground confirmation email is in hand.
    D1
    Ventura → Big Sur
    PCH north through Malibu · Hwy 1 through SLO · Ragged Point → Big Sur
    Hotel + camp scan265 mi · 4.5 hrs
    Highlights
    Piedras Blancas elephant seals — thousands of seals right at the road pull-off near San Simeon, no fee, no crowds compared to tourist spots.
    Pfeiffer Beach — purple sand from manganese garnet; take the unsigned Sycamore Canyon Rd off Hwy 1 (easy to miss).
    McWay Falls — 10-min walk to an 80-ft waterfall that drops directly into an inaccessible turquoise cove.
    Eat & Drink
    Firestone Grill, SLO ↗ — tri-tip sandwich, cash only, locals swear by it. Great mid-drive lunch stop.

    Nepenthe ↗ — cliff-top deck 800 feet above the ocean, one of the most dramatic restaurant settings in California. Opened in 1949 on the site of an Orson Welles cabin. The Ambrosia Burger is genuinely good; the sunset from the terrace is unforgettable. Yes it's known — it earned it. Arrive for sunset drinks, stay for dinner.
    Tesla Note
    Charge at San Luis Obispo Supercharger (~175 mi from Ventura) before the Big Sur gap. No charging in Big Sur — arrive with >100 mi remaining. If the overnight becomes Cambria/Monterey instead, this leg gets much easier.
    Start charge: 100%
    SLO stop: ~35%
    Arrive Big Sur: ~65% (after charge)
    Sleep: Refundable hotel/private lodging first; Pfeiffer/Kirk Creek only if cancellation appears
    D2
    Big Sur → Monterey
    Bixby Bridge · Point Lobos · Ocean View Blvd · Pacific Grove
    Hotel75 mi · 2 hrs
    Highlights
    Bixby Bridge — leave camp by 7am and you'll have the bridge nearly to yourself. Golden light, no tour buses.
    Point Lobos State Reserve — best snorkeling in CA, sea otters, harbor seals, cormorant colonies. Arrive at 8am when it opens. $35/car but worth every cent.
    Ocean View Blvd, Pacific Grove — free coastal drive with the same sea stacks, tide pools, and Lone Cypress views that 17-Mile Drive charges $12 for. Runs right along the water through Pacific Grove, ends at Lovers Point. Skip the toll road.
    Eat & Drink
    Monterey Fish House ↗ — 2114 Del Monte Ave, one block from the water. Cash only, no reservations, usually a line — which tells you everything. Best fresh fish in Monterey, zero tourist-trap energy. Order whatever the day's catch is.

    Passionfish, Pacific Grove ↗ — dinner option if you want something more elevated. Farm-to-sea sourcing, genuinely excellent seafood, frequently cited as the best restaurant on the Monterey Peninsula. Reservations advised.
    Tesla Note
    Monterey Supercharger at the Del Monte Shopping Center. Easy, central location. Charge to 90%+ here — you'll want range for the SF approach.
    Sleep: Hotel, Pacific Grove or Cannery Row area
    Tip: Pacific Grove is cheaper than Monterey and walkable

    D4
    SF → Point Reyes → Bodega Bay
    Muir Beach · Bear Valley · Drake's Beach · Tomales Bay · Bodega Bay
    Flex90 mi · 3 hrs
    Highlights
    ★ Hog Island Oyster Co. — the definitive West Coast oyster experience. Reserve a BBQ picnic table on Tomales Bay weeks ahead ($5/person, includes gloves, shucking knife, condiments). You eat oysters grown in the water you can see 20 feet away. Bring Champagne, sourdough, butter, and a spare afternoon. Or walk up to The Boat bar (Fri–Mon) for shucked-to-order without a reservation.
    Point Reyes Lighthouse — 300 steps down to a dramatic perch over the Pacific. Wildest wind you'll ever feel. Worth every step.
    Eat & Drink
    Station House Cafe, Point Reyes Station ↗ — local institution since 1964, recently renovated. Seasonal farm-to-table menu, great brunch, full bar, garden patio. The go-to for anyone who actually lives in West Marin. Closed Wednesdays.

    Bodega Bay: Spud Point Crab Company ↗ — order at the window, eat on the dock, watch the fishing boats. Crab cakes, chowder, crab sandwiches.
    Tesla Note
    Charge at Petaluma or Santa Rosa Supercharger on the way (10 min off route on US-101). Bodega Bay has no Supercharger — arrive with enough to reach Fort Bragg the next day (Hwy 1 north is slow and has no chargers).
    Sleep: Doran/Bodega Dunes if available; otherwise Petaluma, Santa Rosa, or Bodega hotel
    Camp note: Holiday spillover night, so keep hotel backup live
    D5
    Bodega Bay → Fort Bragg
    Jenner · Sea Ranch · Gualala · Point Arena · Mendocino · Fort Bragg
    Hotel100 mi · 3 hrs
    Highlights
    Jenner — pull over at the mouth of the Russian River. Harbor seals everywhere, pelicans, the green river meeting the blue ocean. 15-minute stop that you'll remember forever.
    Sea Ranch Chapel — tiny modernist masterpiece, open to anyone. Feels like being inside a piece of sculpture.
    Glass Beach, Fort Bragg — old town dump became a beach of polished sea glass. Wear shoes; the glass is beautiful but sharp at edges.
    Eat & Drink
    Mendocino: Ravens Restaurant — farm-to-table in a Victorian inn, excellent even if you're not vegetarian. Dinner option.

    Noyo Fish Company ↗ — right on Noyo Harbor, the actual fishing harbor of Fort Bragg. 4.7 stars, dock-fresh catch, best clam chowder on the North Coast. Order at the window, eat outside watching the fishing boats unload. The salmon tacos are exceptional.

    North Coast Brewing Co. ↗ — for a beer after dinner. Old Rasputin Imperial Stout is one of the great American stouts. The food here is secondary to the beer.
    Tesla Note
    Critical leg. Ukiah Supercharger is 35 mi inland off US-101 — worth the detour if leaving Bodega Bay at less than 80%. Alternatively, Fort Bragg has several Level 2 destination chargers (hotels, city lot). Charge overnight at hotel.
    Sleep: Hotel Fort Bragg or MacKerricher State Park camp (1 mi north of town)

    Fort Bragg → Eureka
    Leggett · Hwy 101 north · Avenue of the Giants · Humboldt Redwoods · Eureka
    Hotel155 mi · 3.5 hrs
    Highlights
    At Leggett, Hwy 1 joins US-101 north — the coast gives way to redwoods. This is the transition.
    Chandelier Drive-Through Tree — $10, kitschy, but driving a Tesla through a living 2,400-year-old tree is a moment.
    Founders Grove, Humboldt Redwoods SP — free, short 1-mile loop, contains trees among the largest living things on earth. Stand next to them. No photo prepares you.
    Eat & Drink
    Sea Grill ↗ — #2 of 160 restaurants in Eureka on TripAdvisor with 600+ reviews, open since 1988. Fresh local seafood and steak in a historic Old Town building, live guitar, genuinely excellent. Open Tue–Sat 5–9pm, reserve ahead.

    Brick & Fire Bistro ↗ — #1 in Eureka, 1,100+ Yelp reviews, featured on Diners Drive-Ins and Dives. Wood-fired Mediterranean: pizzas, pastas, stuffed calamari, mushroom cobbler. Lively, local, and the real pick if you want something more casual with a great wine list.
    Tesla Note
    Willits Supercharger (~65 mi north of Fort Bragg). Then Garberville area has a charger. Then Fortuna Supercharger near Eureka. This is actually the best-covered rural stretch — don't stress it.
    Sleep: Hotel, Eureka Old Town
    Tip: Old Town Eureka has great Victorian architecture — walk it in the evening

    Camp105 mi · 2.5 hrs
    Highlights
    Trinidad — fishing village of 300 people with a stunning protected harbor. Walk to the lighthouse viewpoint, eat at Seascape Restaurant on the pier. One of the most beautiful small towns in California.

    D8
    Crescent City → Bandon, OR
    Oregon border · Boardman Corridor · Pistol River · Gold Beach · Bandon
    Camp175 mi · 4 hrs
    Highlights
    Boardman State Scenic Corridor — 40 miles of the most dramatic coastal scenery in Oregon with almost no tourists. Natural Bridges viewpoint, Thomas Creek Bridge (Oregon's highest bridge), Arch Rock. Stop at every viewpoint.
    Face Rock Beach, Bandon — arrive at golden hour. Sea stacks rise out of the water like sleeping giants. This is the image of Oregon's coast.
    Eat & Drink
    Gold Beach: Barnacle Bistro ↗ — casual seafood on the harbor, good fish tacos and clam chowder.

    Bandon: Tony's Crab Shack ↗ — right on the harbor, eat outside, watch the fishing boats, great crab and fish options. Very local, zero pretension.
    Tesla Note
    Gold Beach (Brookings area) has Level 2 destination chargers — stop there for 30–45 min while eating lunch. Coos Bay Supercharger is 30 miles north of Bandon — you can charge the morning of Day 9 instead. Arrive Bandon camp with ~40% and you're fine.
    Sleep: Bullards Beach State Park, Bandon (or hotel in town for a treat)
    D9
    Bandon → Newport
    Coos Bay · Shore Acres · Cape Arago · Oregon Dunes · Heceta Head · Florence · Newport
    Camp or Hotel150 mi · 3.5 hrs
    Highlights
    Shore Acres State Park — dramatic clifftop park with formal botanical gardens. Completely unexpected and stunning. $5 day use.
    Heceta Head Lighthouse — most photographed lighthouse on the Oregon coast. The keeper's quarters is a B&B — book if you want an unforgettable night there instead of camp.
    Heceta Head to Cape Perpetua viewpoints — instead of Sea Lion Caves ($14/person elevator into a cave where sea lions are outside in summer anyway), pull over at the free cliff-top overlooks between Heceta Head and Yachats. Watch sea lions from above for free.
    Eat & Drink
    Café Mundo, Coos Bay — local coffee shop, good breakfast sandwiches.

    Local Ocean Seafoods, Newport ↗ — sources directly from 60+ fishing vessels docked across the street on Yaquina Bay. Floor-to-ceiling windows, fishermen unloading below you. Travelers' Choice 2025, #5 of 70 in Newport. Reserve on Tock or arrive early as a walk-in. Order whatever landed that morning.

    Rogue Ales Public House ↗ — for a beer. Dead Guy Ale, original location on the bayfront. The setting is great; go for drinks, not a seafood dinner.
    Tesla Note
    Coos Bay Supercharger — charge here in the morning before leaving. Florence has Level 2 options. Newport has destination chargers at several hotels; Beverly Beach SP camp has no charging (arrive with 30%+).
    Sleep: Beverly Beach State Park (camp) or Hotel Sylvia, Newport (book-themed hotel, legendary)
    D10
    Newport → Cannon Beach
    Yaquina Head · Depoe Bay · Cape Perpetua · Lincoln City · Tillamook · Cape Lookout · Cannon Beach
    Hotel130 mi · 3 hrs
    Highlights
    Yaquina Head Outstanding Natural Area — best accessible tide pools in Oregon (Cobble Beach), plus the lighthouse and murre colonies nesting on the basalt stacks. $7/car, arrive at low tide.
    Cape Perpetua — Thor's Well (drains into the ocean at high tide, terrifying and beautiful), Spouting Horn, Cook's Chasm. Oregon's most dramatic tidal feature.
    Cape Lookout hike — 5 mi round trip to a clifftop jutting into the ocean, whale watching.
    Eat & Drink
    Nevør Shellfish Farm, Netarts Bay ↗ — the Oregon oyster stop. Buy a dozen direct from the farm for ~$10, eat at picnic tables overlooking Netarts Bay. The flavor is distinct from Hog Island — intensely briny, a burst of salt that mellows to a clean mineral finish. Completely different from the CA oysters. Call ahead: (503) 842-2741.

    Depoe Bay: Tidal Raves ↗ — built into the cliff, waves crashing below your table. Pricey but the setting earns it. Good for a long lunch.

    Tillamook Creamery ↗ — genuinely excellent cheese, great ice cream. Tillamook is one of the great American farmer co-ops. Stop for 20 minutes, eat a scoop, buy a block of sharp cheddar for the road.
    Tesla Note
    Lincoln City Supercharger — convenient right off Hwy 101. Charge here mid-day. Cannon Beach has Level 2 chargers near the shops and a few hotels have charging. Good coverage for this leg.
    Sleep: Hotel, Cannon Beach
    Tip: Stay 1 block from the beach — Haystack Rock sunrise requires a 5-min walk

    Cannon Beach → Astoria
    Haystack Rock · Ecola State Park · Seaside · Astoria
    Hotel30 mi · 45 min
    Highlights
    Haystack Rock at sunrise — yes, everyone does this. At 6am in summer you'll share it with a handful of photographers and a colony of tufted puffins nesting in the cracks. It earns its fame.
    Ecola State Park — Crook Point and Indian Beach have the view of the Tillamook Rock Lighthouse that you've seen in every Oregon photo. Less visited than Cannon Beach.
    Astoria Column — climb 164 steps, launch a cedar toy biplane from the top. Best panoramic view of the Columbia River.
    Eat & Drink
    Astoria: Fort George Brewery ↗ — one of the best craft breweries in the Pacific Northwest, housed in a 1924 building. Their Suicide Squeeze IPA is outstanding.

    Bridgewater Bistro ↗ — proper waterfront dinner, Columbia River views, consistently excellent seafood (seared salmon, Dungeness crab, albacore). Local favorite, not a tourist spot. Confirmed open and well-reviewed into 2026.
    Tesla Note
    Astoria has destination chargers at the Cannery Pier Hotel and a few others. No Supercharger yet in Astoria as of 2025 — check the Tesla app before arrival. Seaside Supercharger is only 15 min south. Portland has many Superchargers for the return.
    Sleep: Cannery Pier Hotel (built on a pier over the Columbia River, iconic) or Hotel Elliott (Victorian, downtown Astoria)

    California: Central Coast
    Central Coast
    ★ Piedras Blancas Elephant Seal Rookery ↗
    Near San Simeon, Hwy 1 · Free
    A roadside pull-off where thousands of elephant seals haul out to breed, molt, and laze on the beach, year-round. The males are the size of pickup trucks. In summer you'll see juveniles sparring. Docents on-site. No fee, no reservation, most visitors drive right past it.
    Pull off at the signed turnout 4.5 miles north of Hearst Castle entrance. Walk to the wooden boardwalks above the beach. Summer has juveniles; winter has pups.

    Big Sur
    ★ Pfeiffer Beach — Purple Sand ↗
    Big Sur · $12/car · Easy to miss
    The sand here turns purple-black from manganese garnet in the clifftop rocks. There's a dramatic sea arch at the north end of the beach. The turn-off (Sycamore Canyon Rd) is an unsigned narrow road — Hwy 1 signs specifically say "No RVs" — and most people miss it entirely.
    The turn is 0.3 miles south of the Big Sur post office, on the ocean side. Look for a small brown sign and a narrow road going down through cypress trees. About 2 miles down to the parking lot.

    California: North Coast — Marin to Mendocino
    Marin
    ★ Hog Island Oyster Co. — Tomales Bay Farm ↗
    Marshall, CA · Reserve BBQ table weeks ahead · hogislandoysters.com
    The definitive West Coast oyster experience. You reserve a picnic table on the edge of Tomales Bay ($5/person fee includes gloves, shucking knife, and condiments), buy oysters grown in the water directly in front of you, and spend 2–3 hours eating, drinking, and watching the bay. The oysters are consistently superior — off-bottom farmed, cleaner shells, sweeter flavor than the competition. Two time slots: 10:30am and 2pm. Book weeks ahead on weekends.

    Can't get a table? The Boat Oyster Bar (open Fri–Mon, no reservation) shucks and grills oysters for you on a small outdoor menu with local wine and beer.
    Bring: a bottle of Champagne or crisp white wine, sourdough bread, salted butter, a lemon, Tabasco. They supply the tools and condiments but the bread and wine make it transcendent. This is the one food experience on the trip worth planning months around.

    ★ Cape Perpetua — Thor's Well ↗
    Near Yachats, OR · $5 day use · Visit 1 hr before high tide
    Thor's Well is a hole in the basalt shelf that appears to drain the ocean — at the right tide, waves surge in and the water drains through a subterranean passage below. Spouting Horn nearby shoots water 20 feet into the air. The most dramatic tidal feature on the Oregon coast. Timing is everything: arrive 1 hour before high tide.
    Check tide charts before you go. NOAA tide charts for Yachats are accurate. Mid-tide on the incoming is the peak spectacle. It's also genuinely dangerous — stay behind the marked viewing areas.

    Car Camping Setup — Model Y
    Inside the Car
    Sleeping surface: Fold rear seats flat. The Model Y's trunk floor is at a slight angle — a 3" or 4" memory foam topper levels it out and adds comfort. Cut to fit or buy a pre-cut Model Y specific pad (Tesmat, EcoFlow, etc.).

    Dimensions: ~75" length × 42" wide at narrowest. Two adults fit — it's cozy but doable. Sleep head-toward-front.

    Climate: Enable Camp Mode before sleeping. Set to 68°F. On the Oregon/Northern CA coast in summer, nights are 50–60°F — Camp Mode barely needs to run.

    Window covers: Essential for privacy and darkness. Reflectix cut to fit each window works well. Also insulates. Take measurements in advance.
    Camp Mode Details
    How to enable: Climate Controls → Fan icon → Camp. Or via Tesla app. Available when plugged in or when battery is above ~20%.

    Battery use: ~1–1.5% per hour at mild temps (~60°F). Overnight 8 hrs = ~10–12% drain. In warmer weather (if you need A/C) it can be 15–20%.

    Interior light: Camp Mode keeps the interior ambient lights on at low level. You can adjust or turn off.

    Do not leave below 20%: The car will disable Camp Mode at 20% to preserve driving range. Plan accordingly — arrive camp at 50%+ if you want comfort overnight without stress.
    Campground noise etiquette: Camp Mode runs the climate system quietly — you won't bother your neighbors. The car is nearly silent. Neighbors may be curious about the Tesla at camp; it's a good conversation starter.
