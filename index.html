Best Default Route

Go north on the coast for the drama, turn inland for Bend and Crater Lake, then come home through Southern Oregon and the redwoods.

  • Outbound: Ventura, Big Sur, Mendocino or Avenue of the Giants, Redwood National and State Parks, Brookings.
  • Oregon coast: Samuel H. Boardman, Bandon, Cape Perpetua, Yachats/Newport, Cannon Beach, Astoria.
  • Interior: Portland, Columbia River Gorge, Hood River, Bend, Crater Lake, Ashland.
  • Return: Redwoods or Mount Shasta, then pick a coastal or I-5 sprint depending on time.

Planning Rules

Use hotels when they unlock dinner, laundry, showers, or a short walk to town. Use camp when you can wake up at a beach, redwood grove, lake, or dark-sky site.

  • Book Oregon State Park campgrounds at the six-month window, especially Harris Beach, Cape Blanco, South Beach, and Fort Stevens.
  • Keep Crater Lake flexible until July if Rim Drive matters; snow can delay full access.
  • Arrive at camp with enough battery to run Camp Mode overnight and still reach the next charger.
  • Before departure, check TripCheck, Caltrans QuickMap, wildfire smoke, and charger availability.

Model Y Camping + Charging

Practical constraints for sleeping in the car without turning the trip into a charger hunt.

Camp Mode protocol

  • Camp Mode maintains cabin temperature and powers USB/low-voltage outlets while parked. Set climate before sleep and keep the phone paired.
  • Because Sentry Mode, vehicle alarm, and Walk-Away Door Lock are inactive in Camp Mode, manually lock the car from the screen or app before sleeping.
  • Plan overnight energy conservatively. In mild coastal weather, budget roughly 8 to 15 percent. In cold mountain weather, budget more.
  • Pack window shades, bug screens, a fitted mattress, small fan, condensation towel, headlamps, and two small duffels instead of hard bins in the sleep area.

Charging rhythm

  • Morning: leave camp with enough buffer to reach breakfast plus a fast charger.
  • Midday: charge during lunch in larger towns: Eureka/Arcata, Brookings, Coos Bay, Newport, Seaside/Astoria, Portland, Hood River, Bend, Medford.
  • Crater Lake: Mazama Village has an EV station listed by NPS, but do not make it the only plan. Charge in Klamath Falls, Medford, or Bend first.
  • Remote coast: Samuel Boardman, Cape Blanco, and Cape Perpetua are view-first zones. Treat them as charged-arrival areas.
